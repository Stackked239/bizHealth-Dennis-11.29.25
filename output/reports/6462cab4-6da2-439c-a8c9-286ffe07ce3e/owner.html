2. Key Findings

The comprehensive Phase 2 analysis identified five critical insights that explain EWM Global's current position and define the path forward:

Finding 1: Governance Is the Root Constraint

The absence of external governance enables all other problems to persist.

EWM Global operates without an advisory board and with minimal board oversight (rated 2/5), despite 24 years of operation and $24M in revenue. This governance void creates strategic blind spots, delays critical decisions, and removes the accountability that would force action on known issues like cash management, margin improvement, and sales efficiency.

> "Without external accountability, internal issues remain internal."

Finding 2: Financial Position Cannot Support Growth Ambitions

The company's 20% growth target is mathematically impossible given current capital structure.

| Growth Requirement | Current Capacity | Gap | |:-------------------|:-----------------|:----| | CAC investment needed for 20% growth | $3.7 million | â€” | | Available working capital | $1.5 million | $2.2M shortfall | | Cash ratio (benchmark: 1.2) | 0.43 | 64% below benchmark | | Gross margin (benchmark: 42%) | 32% | 10 points below |

Without external capital, maximum sustainable growth is 12-15%â€”well below stated targets.

Finding 3: Sales Conversion Is the Primary Revenue Leak

90% of sales effort produces zero revenue due to a 10% close rate.

The company's sales velocity index of 25.93 (versus 68 benchmark) indicates fundamental sales process dysfunction. Combined with a 270-day sales cycle, this means:

  • Each lost deal represents $70,000 in potential revenue
  • Approximately $1.5-2.5 million in annual revenue goes unrealized
  • Competitive differentiation clarity at 3/5 prevents effective value articulation

Finding 4: Organizational Dysfunction Undermines Execution

Culture (2/5) and decision-making (2/5) scores indicate an organization unable to execute change.

With only 4 executives managing 170 employees across 3 locations (1:42.5 ratio versus 1:25 best practice), the organization suffers from:

  • Decision bottlenecks that extend sales cycles and response times
  • Cultural fragmentation across locations
  • Change resistance that blocks technology adoption
  • Employee compliance without commitment (3/5 engagement)

Finding 5: Massive Technology Investment Produces Subpar Returns

$2.5M annual technology spend (10.4% of revenue) yields 50% equipment utilization and 3/5 automation.

The company has built infrastructure for 2-3x its current scale but directed technology investment toward client products rather than internal operations. This creates a "cobbler's children" problem where innovation culture (4/5) benefits customers but not internal efficiency.
