EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: EWM GLOBAL STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT

BUSINESS OVERVIEW

EWM Global is a 24-year-old financial services company generating $24M in annual revenue with 15% year-over-year growth. The company serves the financial services sector with 85 employees, delivering specialized services that command premium pricing ($70K average deal size) and exceptional customer loyalty (98% retention rate, $1M lifetime value).

Current Position: The company stands at a critical inflection point - profitable and growing on the surface, but suffering from severe organizational weaknesses that threaten long-term sustainability. Without immediate intervention, EWM faces an 18-month window before its competitive advantages erode and growth stalls.

KEY FINDINGS

1. The Excellence Paradox

EWM has built "islands of excellence" - world-class customer retention, strong technology infrastructure, perfect compliance scores - without the organizational bridges to connect them. The company delivers exceptional external results while failing internally, creating an unsustainable dependence on individual heroics rather than scalable systems.

2. Critical Leadership & Culture Crisis

With culture scores at 2/5 and leadership effectiveness at 2.67/5 (both bottom quartile), the organizational foundation is crumbling. This dysfunction cascades through every aspect of operations, threatening employee retention, decision-making speed, and the sustainability of customer excellence.

3. Revenue Engine Breakdown

Despite premium positioning, the sales engine is fundamentally broken: 270-day sales cycles (7x industry standard), 10% close rates, and 1% marketing conversion. This creates severe cash flow pressure and constrains growth despite strong market demand.

4. Hidden Profit Potential

Current gross margins of 32% trail the 42% benchmark by 10 points, representing $2.6M in unrealized annual profit. Combined with 50% equipment utilization and limited process automation, the company has significant untapped efficiency gains available.

5. False Security Risk

Strong financial metrics and customer loyalty mask fundamental weaknesses. The company operates without an advisory board, with inadequate cash reserves (1.5 months), and depends heavily on founder relationships - creating a "house of cards" vulnerable to sudden collapse.
