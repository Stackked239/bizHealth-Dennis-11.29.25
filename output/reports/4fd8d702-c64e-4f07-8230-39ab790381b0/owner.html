EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: EWM GLOBAL TRANSFORMATION IMPERATIVE

BUSINESS OVERVIEW

EWM Global is a 24-year-old fintech services company specializing in employee benefits administration, with 170 employees across 3 locations. The company has built exceptional capabilities in compliance (4.14/5), technology infrastructure (4.57/5), and customer relationships (98% retention, $1M lifetime value).

Current Position: Despite world-class operational capabilities and loyal customers, EWM Global faces imminent collapse with only 1.1 months of cash remaining and monthly losses of $1.33M. The company represents a paradox: exceptional strengths neutralized by catastrophic leadership and financial failures.

KEY FINDINGS

1. Financial Emergency - 45 Days to Insolvency

Monthly burn exceeds revenue by 61% ($3.5M costs vs $2.17M revenue), creating an existential crisis. Without immediate intervention, the company will cease operations within 45-60 days. Contradictory financial reporting suggests deeper governance issues.

2. Leadership Crisis Paralyzing Response

Leadership effectiveness (2.67/5) and decision-making capability (2/5) have created organizational paralysis. After 24 years without an advisory board, insular thinking prevents the urgent action required. This dysfunction cascades through all operations.

3. World-Class Assets Trapped by Poor Execution

The company achieves 98% customer retention and generates $1M in lifetime value per customer, yet operates at 32% gross margins (vs. 42% benchmark). Equipment utilization sits at 50% despite $2.5M annual technology investment.

4. Toxic Culture Undermining All Efforts

Company culture scores lowest across all dimensions (2/5), creating an invisible brake on improvement initiatives. Despite good retention (19.9%), productivity remains 12% below benchmark, and innovation is stifled.

5. Business Model Fundamentally Broken

The 270-day sales cycle and 10% close rate limit growth to 31 new customers annually - insufficient for sustainability. Combined with below-market margins and high cost structure, the current model cannot generate positive cash flow.
