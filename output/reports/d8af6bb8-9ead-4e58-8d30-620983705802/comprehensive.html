Current Position

Hopcraft Brewing Co. represents a strategic paradox: a company with exceptional customer fundamentals trapped by inadequate infrastructure. After eight years of operation, the brewery has achieved what many competitors cannotgenuine customer loyalty, authentic brand culture, and marketing efficiency that outperforms 95% of the industry. Yet revenue declined 3% against a 12% growth target, not due to weak demand, but because the company physically cannot produce more beer.

The Core Reality: Hopcraft is a demand-rich, supply-constrained business operating at the limits of its infrastructure. The company has outgrown its operational foundation while maintaining startup-era systems across technology, human resources, and organizational structure.

2. Key Findings

Finding #1: The Capacity-Capital Trap

The company is caught in a self-reinforcing constraint cycle that cannot be broken through incremental improvements.

Production operates at 88-92% utilization across space, equipment, and personnel. This ceiling prevents revenue growth, which limits profit generation, which constrains investment capacity, which perpetuates the capacity ceiling. The -3% revenue decline is not a demand problemcustomers want to buy more. It is a supply problem that requires coordinated intervention.
